About Me

I am many things. I am a middle child of seven from the Midwest. I am a lifelong student, a puzzle solver, a story teller, and I am gifted with the ability to comfortably talk with anyone. Aside from my family, my two most favorite things are language and travel. I have studied four languages and have begun a fifth. Traveling has allowed me to explore my love of languages at a deeper level. I love learning about cultures, and I love to hear the stories of others. For the past year, I have had the privilege of traveling twenty-six countries while living out of a backpack. Exploring so many different places and cultures has provided me a new perspective on humanity. I find more beauty in the differences among humans. I consider this a sort of salvation, and I wouldn't trade my experiences for anything.

Re-entry

Now, I find myself in Denver working towards the title of Full Stack Web Developer. After being on the move for so long, I find it difficult to stay put. It took me some time to adjust to my new schedule. So how can I be successful without some sort of tool to keep my mind focused on my new goal? Fortunately, there are some simple tools that, if properly deployed, create a more organized and efficient me. Taking a few minutes each morning to set the stage for my day allows me to time-manage with ease, accomplishing my goals with precision. Goal setting is key in this process. A list of attainable goals, creates a mindset of determination. Be it daily, short-term goals or that goal off in the distance, checking off those to-do's brings pride and discipline, and when one is proud of the work being accomplished, procrastination doesn't stand a chance.
